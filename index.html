Physalis Seeds

Complete Guide for Growing Physalis peruviana

Seed Details

Collection Date: 15 June 2025

Source: Ripe fruit bought from Lidl, Austria

Storage Advice: Store seeds in a cool, dry place in an airtight container. Avoid direct sunlight and excessive moisture to maintain viability for up to 1 year.

Quick Facts

Scientific NamePhysalis peruviana
FamilySolanaceae
Plant TypePerennial in warm climates, annual in cooler climates
Fruit ColorGolden orange
TasteSweet, tangy, tropical
UsesEaten fresh, in jams, desserts, salads

Planting Instructions

  1. Seed Preparation: Physalis seeds do not require cold stratification. Sow directly indoors in seed trays 6–8 weeks before the last frost date.
  2. Sowing Depth: Plant seeds about 0.5 cm deep in moist, well-draining seed compost.
  3. Germination: Keep soil at 20–25°C and ensure consistent moisture. Germination usually takes 7–14 days.
  4. Transplanting: Once seedlings have 2–3 true leaves, transplant into larger pots or directly into the garden after frost risk has passed.
  5. Spacing: Plant 45–60 cm apart in full sun.

Care & Maintenance

  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun for maximum fruit production.
  • Watering: Keep soil evenly moist but not waterlogged.
  • Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4–6 weeks during the growing season.
  • Support: Use stakes or cages to support heavy fruit-laden branches.
  • Pest Control: Watch for whiteflies, aphids, and spider mites.

Fun Fact

Physalis, also known as Cape Gooseberry or Goldenberry, is wrapped in a papery husk that acts as a natural protective packaging, keeping the fruit fresh for weeks without refrigeration.
